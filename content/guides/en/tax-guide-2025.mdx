# Complete Guide to Korean Taxes for Expats (2025)

Living in Korea means dealing with the **National Tax Service (NTS)**. Whether you are an English teacher, an IT professional, or a business owner, understanding your tax obligations is crucial.

## ðŸ“‹ Do I need to file taxes?

If you have stayed in Korea for **more than 183 days**, you are considered a **resident** for tax purposes.

This means you are taxed on your worldwide income (with some exceptions for short-term residents).

### Year-End Tax Settlement (Yeonmal Jeongsan)

Most employees go through the **Year-End Tax Settlement in February** handled by their company.

**Process:**
- Download simplified documents from the Hometax website
- Submit the documents to your company
- Company processes the final settlement

Key deductions that can reduce your tax burden:

### 1. Credit Card Usage
- **15-30% deduction** depending on usage
- Debit cards have higher deduction rates

### 2. Medical Expenses
- Deductible if **exceeding 3% of total income**
- Includes expenses for yourself and dependents

### 3. Education
- For yourself and dependents
- Includes university, language schools, etc.

### 4. Donations
- To registered charities
- Receipt required
